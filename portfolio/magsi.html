Overview

Making group activities fun!

Our team (of three) was provided with some initial requirement analysis data from the SMARTACT team. The requirements were asking us to devise a solution for motivating members in a family to be more active so as to have a healthy lifestyle.

Context

SMARTACT is an interdisciplinary research project involving international experts and research groups from three German universities. It aims to improve health behavior by behavior change interventions. As a sub-project, SMARTMOBIILITY is a toolbox for enabling contextual real-time interventions. In this context, MAGSI makes use of the data collected and stored by the SMARTMOBILITY system to offer a playful way of improving the amount of activity performed by a family.

Idea

Applications aiming to support and motivate people to be more active are a common sight in today’s app stores. Most of the fitness applications currently available don’t only provide functions like tracking activities, but also enable the user to compare their results to those of others. Thereby competition is promoted to generate motivation. By focusing on competition as the only tool of social interaction, the possibilities of generating motivation by working together instead of against each other are missed. This is the origin of the idea behind MAGSI: How can the synergies that arise from contributing to a common goal be utilized to encourage people to be more active?

User Journey

We sketched two scenarios representing the system process and the emotional impact that the system should eventually create. We realized it was necessary to define the system flow before diving into the development phase.

Scenario 1: Reviewing last week's results and Setting new goals

Reviewing last week’s result

It’s a rainy Sunday afternoon. Family Miller has gathered in the living room to enjoy the last hours of the weekend together. They want to see if they reached the common goal they set last week: Were they as active as they wanted to be? The son, Bob, therefore starts the MAGSI system and the visualization of last week’s results is shown. The Millers proudly see that they actually reached their goal.


Setting the weekly goal

The Millers have gathered around MAGSI and proudly reviewed their results from last week. They feel motivated to set a higher goal and be even more active in the upcoming week. Everybody decides about their individual contribution and finally, they can see how much they will have to do to reach their goal. Full of anticipation of reaching the next goal, they decide to take their pet dog Charlie out for a walk together.


Scenario 2: Being motivated during the week

John Miller returns from work at 6 pm after a typical office day. He walks by MAGSI and is amazed to see that his children have made significant progress towards reaching the family activity goal. His son Bob runs towards him laughing, points at MAGSI and taunts his dad to take their dog Charlie for the evening walk. John smiles and gets the leash. Later he returns home and is happy to see that by his contribution, his family is one step closer to reaching their goal.

System Concept Statement

Nowadays, career is the principal focus of one’s life which leads to a dormant lifestyle and reduces the time spent with their loved ones. Motivating Activity through Group-goal Setting Interface (MAGSI) addresses this problem by providing a physical interaction for family members . It helps to set family activity goals in a playful setting by bringing everyone together in a common area (ex: Living Room) and deciding on what structure to assemble using tangible objects. The size of the structure they can build depends on the amount of activity each family member is willing to contribute. During the week, family activity completion can be visualized by the growth of the tangible structure. Each time a family member reaches a particular checkpoint, the system rewards them by ejecting a building block. This setting will increase the emotional bonding between family members by achieving goals together .

Wiring Diagram

We analyzed the initial hardware concept for any potential flaws and then designed a detailed wiring diagram. This was necessary so as to decide the physical architecture of the MAGSI along with hardware placement measurements.


The Three Main Modules

As a team of three, we decided to segment MAGSI's architecture into three major modules:

  1. Hardware interfacing (using Python)
  2. Visualization (user interface using HTML5, CSS3, Javascript)
  3. System hardware design (physical product)

I was responsible for the visualization module, although we all worked towards with interchanging responsibilities towards the final goal.

Digital Data Flow

The first two modules (Hardware interfacing and Visualization) has to work in sync with each other. The common data sharing point for these modules was a JSON file stored locally on Raspberry Pi.


Physical Prototype

We created a low-fidelity paper prototype (1:1 scale) so as to gauge any anomalies related to hardware calculations. This helped us to evaluate the design before finalizing dimensions for the product design.

Learnings

Initially while defining the scope of the system, it seemed as if the solution can be developed quite quickly. But along the way, we realized that there are lots of intricate details that need to be addressed so as to provide a smooth experience to the user.

One of the major challenges was to develop a light-weight visualisation module which can respond to hardware change events in real-time. I used web-technologies (HTML5, CSS3, and jQuery) for it. Since the system was running on Raspberry Pi, we had to make the visualizations attractive enough without compromising performance. I learned that CSS3 in itself is a quite powerful technology for implementing animations.

Before working on this project, I used to associate user experience with just the design part of it. However, this project made me realize that the development process is as crucial as the design process in defining a good UX. Writing an efficient and optimized code can greatly impact the response time for the system user, which in turn enhances the overall experience.
